Facts & Stats

Trees are essential to life on Earth. They provide oxygen, absorb carbon dioxide, and support countless ecosystems. Yet, we lose millions of trees every year to deforestation and urban development.

10M Hectares of forest lost annually
2.6T Tons of CO₂ absorbed yearly by forests
300B Potential trees that could be planted
  • One mature tree can absorb up to 48 pounds of carbon dioxide per year
  • Trees reduce urban temperatures by up to 8°C through shade and evapotranspiration
  • Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity
  • Deforestation accounts for 10-15% of global greenhouse gas emissions

How to Help

There are many ways you can contribute to tree preservation efforts, from personal actions to community involvement.

Plant Trees

  • Participate in local tree-planting events
  • Plant native trees in your yard
  • Support urban forestry programs
  • Consider donating to tree-planting organizations

Reduce Impact

  • Choose sustainably sourced paper products
  • Reduce paper and wood consumption
  • Recycle paper and cardboard
  • Support companies with green policies

Educate & Advocate

  • Share knowledge about tree importance
  • Support local conservation policies
  • Join environmental organizations
  • Mentor others about tree care

Care for Trees

  • Properly water and maintain trees
  • Protect trees from pests and disease
  • Mulch around trees to retain moisture
  • Report illegal tree removal
